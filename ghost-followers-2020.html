How Ghost Followers Impact You

In January, Sarah, had 10k followers and 1000 engagements – more precisely 850 likes, 150 comments.

That means a post engagement ratio of 10% – 1k engagements/10k followers

Those are some solid numbers. 

But next month she decided to join the dark side and buy 40k followers.

For her, that looks amazing, right? 50k total followers.

As you can imagine, everyone would be a celebrity if they were able to get real followers like that.

The truth is that they are ghost followers – which means that they aren’t humans and they are not going to engage with the Instagram account. 

So let’s do the math again to show you just how much negative impact these “followers” have.

Sarah still received 1k engagements per post which is good.

But now the engagement (likes, comments) is 1k per 50k followers!

Which means the engagement now is measly 2%.

How valuable do you think Instagram would find this account?

If Instagram sees an account plummet like that, they won’t even think of helping it through its algorithm because it’s not in their best interest.

Instagram profits by advertising to real people. ?

If Sarah’s engagement ratio went from 10% to 2%, the algorithm is automatically notified that the posts are less valuable and Instagram will start to show her posts to fewer people.

Showing her post to fewer people means that the engagement rate will drop.

Then fewer people will see her posts online, and then the engagement drops again and then fewer people will see her again and the engagement will drop again.

It’s a never-ending vicious cycle of snowballing to the bottom. ?
